    Graduate

    Graduate

    GRADUATE PROGRAMS

    The Department of Computer Science offers M.S. and Ph.D. degrees in Computer Science. Both degree programs provide students with advanced training in the fundamental principles and processes of computation. A bioinformatics concentration is available for each degree.
    DID YOU KNOW?

    Since its creation in 2001, the Ph.D. program in computer science has produced more than 190 graduates.

    Graduate Study

    FIND THE RIGHT FIT IN INDUSTRY OR RESEARCH

    Computer Science faculty are actively engaged in a wide variety of research endeavors. Research efforts are concentrated in artificial intelligence and neural nets, bioinformatics, databases, digital image and signal processing, graphics and visualization, networks, parallel and distributed computing, programming languages, simulation and modeling and software engineering.

    GRADUATE FAQS

    M.S. in Computer Science

    The Computer Science M.S. program provides students with advanced training in the fundamental principles, processes and applications of information and computational technologies. This flexible program offers students a wide variety of research and technology areas of focus and allows students to find the right fit in industry or research.

    EXPLORE THIS PROGRAM

    M.S. in Data Science and Analytics

    The M.S. in Data Science and Analytics offers a concentration in Big Data and Machine Learning. This three-semester program trains professionals in the rapidly growing field of data science, which lies at the intersection of computer science, statistical methodologies and a wide range of application domains.

    EXPLORE THIS PROGRAM

    Ph.D. in Computer Science

    The Computer Science Ph.D. program focuses on cutting-edge advances in information technologies related to computer science, data science, cyber-security and bioinformatics. Our doctoral students have opportunities to obtain fellowships through the Molecular Basis of Disease program in computational biomedicine and the Brains and Behavior program in neuroscience.

    Pursuing the Ph.D. program part-time is possible, so working professionals are encouraged to apply. Competitive financial aid is available for full-time Ph.D. students, along with tuition waivers.

    EXPLORE THIS PROGRAM

    Graduate Certificates

    • Gateway to Computer Science

    Gateway to Computer Science

    Gateway to Computer Science

    Georgia State University's Gateway to Computer Science Graduate Certificate program is a one-year bridge program designed specifically for students with a bachelor’s degree in non-computing fields who are eager to the field of computing but lack the foundational skills necessary for success in advanced computer science programs. This program will equip them with essential skills in programming, data structures, algorithms and mathematics, preparing them to succeed in a master’s in computer science (MSCS) program.

    The program serves as a steppingstone, providing students with the knowledge and confidence needed to excel in an MSCS curriculum and to thrive in the tech industry. Upon completing the program, students will receive a Computer Science Bridge certificate and gain direct admission to the MSCS program at GSU, opening doors to advanced career opportunities in technology.

    EXPLORE THIS CERTIFICATE PROGRAM
